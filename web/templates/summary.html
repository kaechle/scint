Steve Jobs, co-founder of Apple Inc., was an iconic figure in the technology and business world, known for his groundbreaking contributions and visionary approach. Born on February 24, 1955, Jobs grew up in California and showed an early interest in electronics and computing.

In 1976, alongside Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne, Jobs founded Apple in his parents' garage. They introduced the Apple I, a groundbreaking personal computer. Their next model, the Apple II, became a massive success, kickstarting the personal computing revolution.

Jobs' tenure at Apple was marked by his focus on design and innovation, leading to products like the Macintosh, which introduced graphical user interfaces to a wider audience. However, in 1985, after a power struggle, Jobs left Apple and founded NeXT, a company specializing in high-end computers. {% include 'sourcelink.html' %} During this period, he also bought The Graphics Group, later renamed Pixar, which became a major animation studio.

In 1996, Apple, then struggling, acquired NeXT, and Jobs returned to the company. {% include 'sourcelink.html' %} He spearheaded a historic turnaround, introducing products like the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad, which reshaped technology and culture. His leadership style, though often described as demanding and meticulous, was also credited for Apple's innovation and design ethos.

Jobs passed away on October 5, 2011, after a battle with pancreatic cancer, leaving behind a legacy as a visionary and a pioneer in the tech industry. His story, from founding Apple in a garage to becoming a figurehead of modern technology, continues to inspire entrepreneurs and innovators worldwide.
